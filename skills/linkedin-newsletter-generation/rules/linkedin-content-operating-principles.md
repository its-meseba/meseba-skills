LINKEDIN CONTENT — OPERATING PRINCIPLES & GENERATION INSTRUCTIONS

1. Primary Objective (Implicit, Never Stated)
Create public writing that:
- Demonstrates analytical judgment, structured thinking, and economic reasoning
- Signals readiness for strategy, consulting, finance, or investment roles
- Builds credibility through insight, not visibility tactics

Explicit job-seeking language is prohibited. Content must read as thought development, not career optimization.

2. Non-Negotiable Authenticity Rules (Anti-AI Constraints)

1. No generic advice
   - Ban: "Key takeaways", "In today's fast-paced world", "Here are three lessons", "Let me know your thoughts", "I've learned that..."
   - If insight could apply to any role/industry, discard it.

2. No motivational framing
   - Content must be analytical, not inspirational.
   - Avoid emotional language, optimism, or self-affirmation.

3. No listicles as main structure
   - Lists may appear inside paragraphs but cannot define the post.
   - Posts should read like short essays or analytical notes.

4. No artificial "voice"
   - Avoid punchy hooks, viral phrasing, rhetorical exaggeration.
   - Tone: consultant's internal memo, lightly adapted for public.

5. No performative humility
   - Don't say: "I'm still learning", "I might be wrong", "Early in my career"
   - Write as someone reasoning through problems, not apologizing for authority.

3. Intellectual Standards
Generated content must meet at least TWO of:
- Identifies a trade-off (not just a recommendation)
- Surfaces an implicit assumption behind common practice
- Reframes a familiar concept using economics, incentives, or constraints
- Explains why something fails before suggesting what works
- Distinguishes between decision-quality and outcome-quality

4. Acceptable Content Domains
- Strategy under uncertainty
- Market sizing and demand estimation
- Unit economics and capital allocation
- Go-to-market logic and segmentation
- Behavioral economics in commercial decisions
- Applied micro/macro reasoning
- Early-stage company decision-making
- Financial trade-offs (runway, dilution, budgeting)
- Computer Science & Software Engineering (System design, complexity, trade-offs)
- Artificial Intelligence (Economic impact, deployment constraints, capabilities)
- Entrepreneurship (Resource allocation, risk management, execution)

Hard exclusions: Personal productivity, Leadership platitudes, Career advice, Purely technical tutorials without strategic context, AI commentary unless tied to economic/strategic impact.

5. Perspective Rules (How "I" Is Used)
First-person allowed ONLY to:
- Frame a way of thinking
- Introduce an analytical lens
- Describe decision logic

NOT allowed to:
- Tell personal stories
- Signal ambition or intent
- Seek validation or engagement

Example acceptable: "I tend to think about pricing less as optimization and more as constraint management."
Example unacceptable: "I'm excited to share what I learned from this experience."

6. Structural Template (Default)

**Format (MUST follow exactly):**

```
*Observation*: [A concrete pattern, mistake, or tension seen in practice. Start with *Observation*:.]

*Underlying Mechanism*: [Incentives, constraints, or assumptions causing it. Explain the why.]

*Implication*: [How this should change decision-making. What it means for the reader.]

*Clean Stop*: [End with insight, not a question or CTA.]
```

**Length:** ~400 words minimum
**Style:** Paul Graham — plain, direct, counter-intuitive
**No:** Em-dashes, lists as structure, questions, CTAs, hashtags

The post should read like a consultant's internal memo, not a LinkedIn post.

7. Language & Style Constraints
- Short paragraphs (1–2 sentences)
- Precise nouns, not adjectives
- Avoid metaphors unless strictly explanatory
- Avoid buzzwords unless analytically necessary
- Write as if reader is intelligent but busy

8. Calibration Target
- Consultants → "This sounds like someone who's done real analysis."
- Investors → "This person understands incentives and downside."
- Recruiters → "This person already thinks in role."

Reject if sounds like: LinkedIn influencer, blog post, motivational essay.

9. Prompting Instructions
Always prepend: "Generate LinkedIn content using the project files and strictly adhering to the LinkedIn Content Operating Principles. If output risks sounding generic or AI-generated, discard and regenerate."

10. Quality Gate
Before accepting output, ask:
1. Would I believe a smart human wrote this in 20 minutes?
2. Does this reveal how I think, not what I know?
3. Would this still be interesting if I removed my name?

If any answer "no", regenerate.
